# Module 11: Intellectual and Developmental Disability (IDD) — Adaptive Fitness and Participation

Many NuroGym users have **intellectual disability (ID)** with or without autism, Down syndrome, or cerebral palsy. This module summarises **community adaptive fitness** themes: **inclusion**, **measurable outcomes**, and **social wellbeing** — drawing on mixed-methods and intervention literature.

---

## 11.1 What “Adaptive Fitness” Means Here

**Adaptive fitness** adjusts **how** exercises are done — equipment, environment, instructions, pacing — so the person can **participate meaningfully** without copying a generic gym programme.

Core ingredients often reported in programmes:

1. **Physical skills** — strength, balance, coordination for daily tasks (carrying, stairs, transfers).  
2. **Cognitive supports** — clear steps, visuals, repetition, predictable structure.  
3. **Motivation and engagement** — music, choice, small groups, positive coaching.  
4. **Generalisation** — skills that transfer to home and community (shopping walks, chores, play).

---

## 11.2 Outcomes Seen in Research (Examples)

Studies and programme evaluations in adults with **IDD** report trends such as:

- **Improved fitness** — strength, balance, cardiovascular endurance (varies by baseline).  
- **Body composition** — some programmes show **BMI** or waist changes over months with combined diet + activity support.  
- **Social interaction** — group formats can increase **peer connection** and **confidence**.  
- **Quality of life** — subjective wellbeing when participation is **voluntary** and **respectful**.

**Caveat:** Study quality and follow-up length vary. NuroCoach avoids over-claiming; we focus on **sustainable habits**.

## 11.3 Barriers NuroCoach Helps Address

| Barrier | Adaptive strategy |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Transport and access** | Home routines, minimal equipment (bands, bodyweight). |
| **Communication** | Short sentences, demos, visual sequences, caregiver co-use. |
| **Sensory overload** | Quiet times, headphones, smaller groups — Module 5. |
| **Executive function** | Fixed weekly template, “minimum workout,” timers — Module 3. |
| **Cost** | Free walking, stairs, water bottles as weights (when safe). |

---

## 11.4 Inclusion and Rights

- Participation in **physical activity is a health and human rights issue** — WHO disability guidelines stress **removing barriers** and **respecting autonomy**.  
- **Supported decision-making:** offer **choices** within safe options (route A vs B, music on/off, indoor vs outdoor when possible).
